Solar Cards

The Sun

The Sun is a massive, glowing sphere of hot plasma at the solar system's center, providing essential energy for life on Earth. Its powerful gravitational pull keeps the planets in orbit and will continue to burn for about another 5 billion years.

Mercury

Mercury, the smallest and innermost planet, orbits the Sun at just 57.9 million kilometers, experiencing extreme temperatures due to its thin atmosphere. It completes a swift orbit around the Sun in just 88 Earth days.

Venus

Venus, Earth's twin in size and composition, is the hottest planet due to its thick, toxic clouds and runaway greenhouse effect. It rotates slowly in the opposite direction, taking 243 Earth days for one rotation and 225 days to orbit the Sun.

Earth

Earth, the third planet from the Sun, uniquely supports life with its optimal distance and diverse ecosystems. It has a dynamic climate system and orbits the Sun in approximately 365.25 days, giving us our days and years.

Mars

Mars, the Red Planet, has the largest volcano and canyon in the solar system and is a prime candidate for future human exploration. It has a thin atmosphere, seasons, polar ice caps, and occasional planet-wide dust storms.

Jupiter

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, is known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm, and its strong magnetic field. Composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, it has at least 79 moons, including the four large Galilean moons.

Saturn

Saturn, the second-largest planet, is renowned for its stunning rings composed of ice and rock particles and has at least 83 moons. Primarily made of hydrogen and helium, its largest moon, Titan, features lakes and rivers of liquid methane and ethane.

Uranus

Uranus, the seventh planet, is an ice giant with a unique sideways rotation and a pale blue color due to its methane-rich atmosphere. It has a faint ring system, 27 known moons, and an extreme axial tilt causing unusual seasonal variations.

>

Neptune

Neptune, the farthest planet from the Sun, is an ice giant with a deep blue color and the fastest winds in the solar system. Composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane, it features large storms and has 14 moons, with Triton being the largest.
